PACIFIC LINE LOGISTICS — GENERAL SHIPPING POLICY (PUBLIC)
Version 3.2 — Updated 2026-03-01

BOOKING
Bookings must be placed at least 3 business days before the cargo ready date.
Late bookings are accepted on a space-available basis and may incur a late
booking fee of USD 75.

CUTOFFS
- SI (shipping instruction) cutoff: 48 hours before vessel ETD.
- VGM cutoff: 24 hours before vessel ETD.
- Container gate-in cutoff: 12 hours before vessel ETD.
Missed cutoffs roll the shipment to the next available sailing.

DELAY COMPENSATION POLICY
Compensation for delays applies only when defined in a customer's service
contract. The standard policy is: delays over 72 hours beyond ETA caused by
carrier-controlled factors qualify for a credit (typically 10% of ocean
freight). Delays caused by port congestion, weather events, customs
inspection, or force majeure never qualify for compensation. Customers without
a service contract are served on a best-effort basis with no compensation.

DANGEROUS GOODS
DG cargo requires approval 7 business days before ETD, with MSDS and DG
declaration. Misdeclared DG cargo results in immediate contract review and
fines passed through to the shipper.

CLAIMS
Cargo damage claims must be filed within 14 days of delivery with photos and
survey report. Maximum liability follows the Hague-Visby rules unless a higher
value was declared and insured.
